Pep Guardiola is reportedly set to step down as manager of Manchester City at the end of the 2025–26 season, potentially ending a transformative ten-year spell that redefined both the club and modern English football.

Multiple reports emerging over the past 24 hours claim that Guardiola will leave the Etihad despite still having one year remaining on his contract. While neither Manchester City nor Guardiola have formally confirmed the decision, sources close to the club suggest preparations are already underway for an official announcement. 

Guardiola arrived at Manchester City in 2016 following hugely successful spells at FC Barcelona and FC Bayern Munich. What followed was arguably the most dominant managerial era in Premier League history.

Under Guardiola, City won:

* six Premier League titles
* multiple domestic cups
* the club's first ever UEFA Champions League Trophy 

His side also became the first team in English football history to win four consecutive Premier League titles, while his tactical innovations heavily influenced football across Europe. 

Reports indicate that former City assistant and ex-Chelsea F.C. manager Enzo Maresca is currently the leading candidate to replace Guardiola, although former City captain Vincent Kompany had also been linked with the role. 

The timing of the reports has intensified emotions among supporters, with City still competing for another Premier League title while fresh off an FA Cup victory. Guardiola himself appeared uncomfortable when questioned publicly about his future after the final, abruptly ending one interview as speculation continued to grow. 

Only days earlier, Guardiola had publicly insisted that he still had a contract with the club and dismissed growing rumours surrounding his departure. 

Whether officially confirmed in the coming days or not, the widespread expectation surrounding Guardiola’s exit has already created the feeling that Manchester City are approaching the end of an era. His influence extended far beyond trophies, reshaping the club’s identity and setting a new benchmark for tactical dominance in English football.

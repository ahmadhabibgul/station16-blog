The decision marks a dramatic return for Neymar, who had been absent from the national team setup for an extended period following a serious knee injury suffered in late 2023. Concerns over recurring fitness problems, inconsistent form, and advancing age had led many supporters and analysts to question whether Brazil would move on from one of the most recognizable players in the country’s football history.

At 34 years old, Neymar is expected to play in what could be the final World Cup appearance of his career. Despite the uncertainty surrounding his physical condition, Brazil’s coaching staff ultimately opted to include the veteran attacker alongside a younger generation of talent led by Vinícius Júnior, Raphinha, and Endrick.

Neymar’s recent return to Santos FC was viewed as an attempt to rebuild fitness and regain consistent playing time ahead of the tournament. While flashes of his technical ability remain evident, questions persist over whether he can sustain the intensity required for a full World Cup campaign.

Ancelotti, who took charge of Brazil ahead of the tournament cycle, previously insisted that squad selections would be based on performance and physical readiness rather than reputation alone. Neymar’s inclusion therefore suggests the coaching staff believes he can still provide value both on and off the pitch.

For Brazil, the decision represents a balance between experience and transition. Neymar remains the nation’s all-time leading scorer and one of the most influential figures in modern Brazilian football, yet the team entering the 2026 tournament is increasingly centered around a younger core expected to define the next era of the national side.

Whether Neymar can still deliver decisive performances on football’s biggest stage remains uncertain. However, his inclusion guarantees that one of the sport’s most polarizing and recognizable figures will once again be central to the World Cup spotlight.

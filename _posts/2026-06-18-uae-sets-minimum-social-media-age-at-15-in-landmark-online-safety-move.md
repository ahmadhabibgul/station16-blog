The United Arab Emirates has announced sweeping new regulations that will prohibit children under the age of 15 from using social media platforms, making it the first country in the Arab world to introduce a nationwide minimum age requirement for social media access. The new rules are part of a broader effort to improve online safety and protect young people from harmful digital content and interactions.

Under the new regulations, children younger than 15 will not be permitted to create, operate, or use personal social media accounts. Platforms operating in the UAE will be required to identify and disable underage accounts while implementing robust age-verification systems to prevent users from bypassing restrictions. Self-declared ages will no longer be considered sufficient for verification.

Teenagers aged 15 and 16 will still be allowed to access social media services, but their accounts will be subject to enhanced safeguards. These measures include age-appropriate content filters, restrictions on interactions with unknown users, parental control features, and tools designed to manage screen time.

The UAE government said the new framework was introduced in response to growing concerns over children's exposure to inappropriate content, unsafe online interactions, excessive social media use, and the collection of personal data by technology companies. Officials emphasized that the goal is not to limit access to technology, but to create a safer digital environment for young users.

Social media companies will have up to 12 months to comply with the regulations. Platforms may use digital identity checks and AI-assisted verification technologies to confirm users' ages and ensure compliance with the new rules. Failure to meet the requirements could result in enforcement action by UAE authorities.

The move places the UAE alongside a growing number of countries seeking to restrict children's access to social media. Governments in Australia, the United Kingdom, and several other nations have recently introduced or proposed similar measures amid concerns about the impact of social media on young people's mental health, privacy, and online safety.

As the regulations enter a transition period, the UAE hopes to establish a new model for balancing technological access with child protection, reinforcing its wider push to strengthen digital safety standards across the country.

After three tense days on the floor, the Senate passed the Infrastructure Reform Act on Thursday afternoon by a bipartisan majority of 67 votes.

The legislation directs $800 billion toward roads, bridges, passenger rail, and clean-energy grid upgrades over ten years — the largest federal infrastructure investment since the Interstate Highway Act of 1956.

President Sullivan is expected to sign the bill at a Rose Garden ceremony on Monday.

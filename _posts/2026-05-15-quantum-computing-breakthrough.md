A team at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology announced Thursday that it had successfully operated a one-thousand-qubit quantum processor with error rates low enough for practical computation.

The achievement, published in the journal Nature, is widely considered a watershed moment for the field.

'We've crossed a threshold,' said lead researcher Dr. Priya Nair. 'This is no longer theoretical physics — this is engineering.' The processor's stability opens pathways to breaking current encryption standards and simulating complex protein folding.

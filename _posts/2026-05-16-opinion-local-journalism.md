Every week, another local newspaper closes its doors.

The economics are brutal and well-documented: print revenues in freefall, digital ad money captured by two platforms, and an audience fragmented across dozens of apps and feeds.

But the loss is not merely sentimental — it is constitutional. When the reporter who covered the planning commission for thirty years is gone, who notices that the mayor's brother won the construction contract?

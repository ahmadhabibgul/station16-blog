Representatives from thirty-two countries gathered in Geneva on Wednesday to sign the landmark Accord on Artificial Intelligence Safety, the first international treaty to impose binding restrictions on military AI.

The accord prohibits autonomous weapons systems capable of selecting and engaging targets without human authorisation, and bans AI-powered mass surveillance systems that lack judicial oversight.

Critics argue the agreement lacks a meaningful enforcement mechanism, but supporters call it a historic first step.

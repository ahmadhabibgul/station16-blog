History was written on the streets of Berlin on Sunday morning.

Elias Kosgei, 24, crossed the finish line at the Brandenburg Gate in one hour, fifty-seven minutes, and forty-four seconds — shattering the world record and becoming the first man to run a sub-two-hour marathon in a sanctioned competitive race.

The crowd of 90,000 erupted as his time appeared on the giant clock overhead. Kosgei collapsed to his knees, then rose to take a victory lap wrapped in the Kenyan flag.

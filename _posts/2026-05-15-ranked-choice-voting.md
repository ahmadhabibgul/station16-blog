In a coordinated legislative push described as unprecedented, ranked-choice voting bills advanced out of committee in five states on the same day: Oregon, Michigan, Colorado, Nevada, and Virginia.

Together those states represent 47 electoral votes and, reformers argue, a critical mass of political will.

'We want every lawmaker in every state to see this is not a fringe idea,' said Leila Osei of the National Electoral Reform Coalition. 'This is a mainstream solution to a mainstream problem.'
